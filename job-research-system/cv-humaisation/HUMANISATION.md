# CV Optimiser - Humanisation Techniques

## Overview
This document provides detailed strategies for removing AI detection patterns from CV content while maintaining professional quality and factual accuracy. Based on research into how tools like Grammarly's AI Humanizer work and what AI detectors look for.

---

## Understanding AI Detection

### What AI Detectors Look For

AI detection tools (GPTZero, Originality.ai, Turnitin, Grammarly) analyze text for these patterns:

1. **Perplexity**: How predictable the text is (AI = very predictable)
2. **Burstiness**: Variation in sentence complexity (AI = consistently uniform)
3. **Linguistic Patterns**: Specific phrases and structures AI models favor
4. **Stylistic Consistency**: Too perfect = suspicious
5. **Content Depth**: Generic vs. specific, lived-in details

### AI Writing Signatures
- Overly formal, consistent tone
- Perfect grammar with no natural variations
- Repetitive sentence structures
- Predictable transitions ("Additionally", "Furthermore", "Moreover")
- Generic descriptions that could apply to anyone
- Lack of personal voice or contextual details
- Overly polished with no "human errors"

## Core Humanisation Strategies

### 1. Sentence Structure Variation

#### The Problem
AI often generates sentences with similar patterns:
```
"Led team of X to deliver Y, resulting in Z."
"Managed process of X to improve Y, achieving Z."
"Developed solution for X to address Y, resulting in Z."
```

#### The Solution - Mix It Up

**Short Punchy Statements (5-10 words)**
```
Results spoke for themselves: 94% client retention.
Team grew from 3 to 15 in 9 months.
We missed zero deadlines that year.
```

**Medium Descriptive (12-20 words)**
```
Rebuilt the entire customer onboarding flow based on feedback from 200+ users.
Partnered with engineering to cut deployment time by half through automated testing.
```

**Long Detailed (25-40 words)**
```
During the company's rapid scaling phase, I led the infrastructure team through a critical migration from monolithic architecture to microservices, which reduced system downtime from 4 hours monthly to less than 15 minutes quarterly while supporting 10x user growth.
```

**Implementation Rule**: In any 5-7 bullet points, include:
- 1-2 short statements
- 3-4 medium statements  
- 1-2 long statements

---

### 2. Natural Language Patterns

#### Remove Robotic Transitions

**AI-Typical (Avoid)**:
- Additionally, ...
- Furthermore, ...
- Moreover, ...
- In addition, ...
- Subsequently, ...

**Human Alternatives**:
- [No transition - just start the sentence]
- This led to...
- The result?
- Building on this...
- What followed was...
- From there...
- That experience taught me...

#### Example Transformation
```
BEFORE (AI-robotic):
"Led product development team. Additionally, managed stakeholder relationships. Furthermore, implemented agile methodologies. Moreover, achieved 95% on-time delivery."

AFTER (Human-natural):
"Led the product development team through a challenging year of rapid growth. Stakeholder management became crucial as we scaled—I spent about 30% of my time aligning expectations across 5 departments. We shifted to agile midway through, which honestly felt chaotic at first but ultimately got us to 95% on-time delivery."
```

---

### 3. Industry Voice & Colloquialisms

#### Use Authentic Industry Language

**Tech/SaaS**:
- "shipped the feature" not "delivered the functionality"
- "user churn" not "customer attrition rate"
- "dogfooding our product" not "internal testing"
- "moved fast and broke things" (if appropriate for startup culture)

**Finance**:
- "in the trenches during market volatility"
- "when the deal almost fell through"
- "the trades that kept me up at night"

**Healthcare**:
- "patient outcomes we were really proud of"
- "the protocol that saved us countless hours"
- "when we finally cracked the workflow issue"

**Consulting**:
- "the deck that sold the engagement"
- "client work that actually moved the needle"
- "the framework we built became their playbook"

#### Implementation
- 1-2 industry colloquialisms per role/section
- Use sparingly—still maintain professionalism
- Should feel natural, not forced

---

### 4. Contextual Details (The "Lived-In" Feel)

#### Generic (AI-typical)
```
"Managed team during period of growth"
```

#### Specific (Human-authentic)
```
"Managed team during the company's Series B scaling phase when we went from 20 to 75 employees in 6 months"
```

#### Add Texture
- **Timeframes with context**: "during the 2022 market correction" vs. "in 2022"
- **Situational details**: "when the CEO left unexpectedly" vs. "during leadership transition"
- **Real challenges**: "despite losing 3 key engineers mid-project" vs. "delivered on time"
- **Learning moments**: "which taught me the hard way about..." vs. "learned about..."

#### Examples
```
GENERIC: "Led team to improve customer satisfaction"

CONTEXTUAL: "Led the team through a tough recovery period after we launched a buggy release in Q1—customer satisfaction had tanked to 3.2/5. We did daily standups, personally responded to every complaint, and by Q3 had climbed back to 4.5/5. That period taught me more about crisis management than any course could."
```

---

### 5. Subtle Imperfections

#### The Paradox of Perfection
Perfect grammar = AI flag. Humans make minor "errors" that aren't really errors.

**Acceptable Human Variations:**
- **Sentence fragments** (used deliberately):
  ```
  "Results? Significant. Customer retention jumped 34% in Q2."
  ```
  
- **Casual constructions**:
  ```
  "I spent most of 2023 building..." vs. "The majority of 2023 was spent building..."
  ```

- **Contractions** (where appropriate):
  ```
  "We'd been struggling with..." vs. "We had been struggling with..."
  ```

- **Minor style variations**:
  ```
  "3 major projects" vs. "three major projects" (inconsistent number formatting is actually human)
  ```

**CRITICAL**: Never compromise factual accuracy for humanisation.

---

### 6. Personal Voice Injection

#### Show Personality Appropriate to Industry/Seniority

**Junior → Mid-Level Voice**:
```
"I joined [Company] not really knowing what to expect from a Series A startup. Turns out, you learn fast when you're one of three people in product. That first year, I touched everything—from user research to launch planning—which gave me a foundation I still draw on."
```

**Senior Voice**:
```
"Joined [Company] to lead the product org through a critical inflection point. We'd grown fast but chaotically, and the team needed structure without losing the scrappy mentality that got us there. Built frameworks that scaled to 40 PMs while maintaining the speed of a 10-person team."
```

**Executive Voice**:
```
"Brought on to transform [Company's] go-to-market strategy during a challenging market contraction. The playbook that worked in 2021 wasn't going to cut it. Led a fundamental rethink of our value prop, pricing model, and sales motion that positioned us for sustainable growth regardless of market conditions."
```

---

### 7. Varied Verb Forms & Tenses

#### Don't Start Every Bullet the Same Way

**AI-Typical (All Past Tense, Same Structure)**:
```
• Led team of 8 engineers...
• Managed stakeholder relationships...
• Delivered 12 product features...
• Improved system performance...
• Achieved 95% uptime...
```

**Human-Natural (Varied Openings)**:
```
• Led a team of 8 engineers through...
• Stakeholder management became crucial when...
• Shipped 12 major features, including...
• The system performance overhaul I drove...
• We maintained 95% uptime despite...
```

#### Mix Active Constructions
- Direct statements: "Led the initiative"
- Participial phrases: "Working closely with sales..."
- Prepositional starts: "During the platform migration..."
- Context setting: "When the founder stepped down..."

## Advanced Techniques

### 8. The "Reality Check" Details

Add small details that prove this is real experience, not generated:

```
GENERIC (AI):
"Led digital transformation initiative that improved efficiency"

REALISTIC (Human):
"Led the digital transformation project—the one where we had to convince 50 field agents who'd been using paper forms for 20 years to adopt tablets. Took 6 months of training sessions, a lot of hand-holding, and honestly, some early failures. But by the end, we'd cut processing time by 60% and even the skeptics were converts."
```

The "reality check" elements:
- Specific resistance points ("50 field agents", "paper forms for 20 years")
- Honest challenges ("a lot of hand-holding", "some early failures")
- Realistic timeline ("6 months")
- Human outcome description ("even the skeptics were converts")

---

### 9. Varied Emphasis Points

Don't emphasize the same part of every sentence:

```
BAD (Same Emphasis Pattern):
• **Led** team to deliver X
• **Managed** stakeholders to achieve Y
• **Drove** initiatives that resulted in Z

GOOD (Varied Emphasis):
• Led team of 8 to deliver **3 major features ahead of schedule**
• Stakeholder alignment proved crucial in **securing $2M additional budget**
• The operational efficiency improvements I drove? **Saved 40 hours per week across the team**
```

---

### 10. Authentic Uncertainty & Learning

Humans admit what they didn't know or had to figure out:

```
"I'd never built a team from scratch before joining [Company], so I learned a lot through trial and error that first year. Some hires were home runs, a few weren't. By the time we hit our stride in year two, I had a much clearer framework for what great looked like in this context."
```

**CAUTION**: Use sparingly. Still need to project competence. Best for:
- Senior+ roles where growth/learning is strength
- Challenging situations that demonstrate resilience
- Skills developed on the job (not core competencies)

## Humanisation Workflow

### Step-by-Step Process

**Input**: AI-enhanced content from Agent 2

**Step 1: Structural Analysis**
- Count sentence lengths
- Identify repeated patterns
- Check transition words
- Assess formality consistency

**Step 2: Add Variation**
- Break up similar structures
- Replace robotic transitions
- Mix sentence lengths
- Vary verb forms and openings

**Step 3: Inject Voice**
- Add 1-2 contextual details per role
- Include appropriate industry language
- Insert subtle personality markers
- Add authentic challenges/learnings (where appropriate)

**Step 4: Reality Check**
- Does this sound like a real person wrote it?
- Are there specific, lived-in details?
- Would I believe this person actually did this?
- Does it pass the "tell me more about this" test?

**Step 5: Preservation Check**
- All metrics maintained?
- All keywords preserved?
- Professional tone intact?
- Factual accuracy unchanged?

**Output**: Humanised content ready for validation

## Examples: Before & After

### Example 1: Product Manager Experience

**BEFORE (AI-Enhanced but Robotic)**:
```
Led cross-functional team of 12 members to deliver 8 major product features over 24 months. Additionally, managed stakeholder relationships across 5 departments. Furthermore, implemented agile methodology that improved delivery velocity by 40%. Moreover, achieved 95% on-time delivery rate while maintaining high quality standards. Subsequently, this resulted in increased customer satisfaction scores from 3.8 to 4.6 out of 5.
```

**AFTER (Humanised)**:
```
Built and led a cross-functional team of 12 through what turned out to be the most intense product phase in company history. We shipped 8 major features over 2 years, but the real challenge was keeping everyone aligned—I spent half my time managing expectations across engineering, sales, design, marketing, and exec leadership.

We shifted to agile midway through (which felt chaotic at first), but it proved crucial for hitting that 95% on-time delivery rate we'd committed to. The velocity improvement of 40% came from getting better at saying no and focusing on what really mattered. Customer satisfaction metrics told the story: we went from 3.8 to 4.6 in 18 months, which honestly felt more impactful than any of the features themselves.
```

**Changes Made**:
- Removed "Additionally", "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Subsequently"
- Added contextual details ("most intense product phase", "I spent half my time")
- Varied sentence lengths (12 words → 42 words → 31 words)
- Injected authentic voice ("which felt chaotic at first", "honestly felt more impactful")
- Maintained all metrics (12 members, 8 features, 24 months, 40%, 95%, 3.8→4.6)

---

### Example 2: Technical Role

**BEFORE (AI-Polished)**:
```
Architected and implemented microservices infrastructure that improved system scalability by 10x. Additionally, reduced deployment time from 4 hours to 30 minutes. Furthermore, established CI/CD pipeline that decreased bugs in production by 70%. Moreover, mentored team of 5 junior engineers on best practices.
```

**AFTER (Humanised)**:
```
When I joined, the monolithic architecture was buckling under growth—we were having outages every other week. Led the migration to microservices over 9 months, which wasn't smooth (we had some memorable 2am debugging sessions) but ultimately gave us 10x scalability headroom.

The deployment improvements came from automating what we'd been doing manually: from 4-hour anxiety-inducing deploys to 30-minute automated pushes that I could run without sweating. Built out the CI/CD pipeline that caught 70% of bugs before they hit production, which let the team sleep better at night. Mentored 5 junior engineers through this transformation—they're now the ones teaching the next wave.
```

**Changes Made**:
- Added real situational context ("outages every other week", "memorable 2am debugging sessions")
- Used authentic language ("wasn't smooth", "anxiety-inducing deploys", "without sweating")
- Varied structures (context setting → outcome → process → impact → growth)
- Maintained all metrics (10x, 4 hours→30 minutes, 70%, 5 engineers)
- Added lived-in details that prove this is real experience

### Example 3: Professional Summary

**BEFORE (Generic AI)**:
```
Accomplished product leader with 10+ years of experience driving innovation in SaaS companies. Proven track record of leading cross-functional teams and delivering results. Strong expertise in agile methodologies, stakeholder management, and data-driven decision making. Seeking senior product management role where I can leverage my skills to drive business growth and create exceptional user experiences.
```

**AFTER (Humanised)**:
```
I've spent the last 10 years building products that people actually use—everything from 0-to-1 experiments at early-stage startups to scaling established platforms at Series C companies. The best part? Watching a feature go from "this will never work" in a whiteboard session to "I can't imagine working without this" in user feedback.

At [Company], I led the team that grew our core product from 10k to 100k users in 18 months. Before that, I learned the hard way at [Startup] how important it is to kill bad ideas early (we pivoted three times before finding product-market fit). Now I'm looking for a senior role where I can bring both the strategic thinking and the execution muscle that comes from having been in the trenches.
```

**Changes Made**:
- Removed all generic buzzwords ("accomplished", "proven track record", "leverage my skills")
- Added specific examples with real stakes
- Included authentic learning moment (the pivots)
- Used conversational tone ("The best part?", "actually use")
- Showed real career arc with specific companies and outcomes
- Ended with forward-looking statement that sounds human

## Red Flags to Avoid

### Don't Sacrifice Quality for Humanisation

❌ **Wrong**:
- Making intentional grammar mistakes
- Using slang inappropriately for industry/seniority
- Being overly casual in formal industries
- Removing important keywords to sound more natural
- Fabricating stories or details

✅ **Right**:
- Natural variations in style while maintaining professionalism
- Industry-appropriate colloquialisms
- Authentic contextual details
- All keywords preserved naturally
- True stories with realistic details

## Testing Humanisation Success

### Self-Check Questions

1. **Would this pass the "tell me more" test?**
   - If interviewer says "tell me about X", can candidate elaborate naturally?
   - Are there enough specific details to support a conversation?

2. **Does it sound like one person wrote it?**
   - Consistent voice across all sections?
   - Similar level of formality/casualness throughout?

3. **Are there "only a human would write this" moments?**
   - Authentic learning experiences?
   - Real challenges mentioned?
   - Contextual details that feel lived-in?

4. **Is it professionally appropriate?**
   - Right tone for industry and seniority level?
   - No inappropriate casualness?
   - Maintains credibility?

5. **Are all facts preserved?**
   - Every metric from Agent 2 still present?
   - Keywords still integrated?
   - No fabricated details?

## Key Takeaway

**The goal is not to trick AI detectors—it's to write content that sounds authentically human because it incorporates the natural variations, contextual richness, and personal voice that real humans naturally use when writing about their real experiences.**

Good humanisation makes the CV more engaging for human readers while avoiding AI detection patterns. It's a win-win when done correctly.
